# PropCRM + PropPulse — Friday Demo Script

**Audience:** UAE real estate brokers / brokerage owners
**Duration:** 5–7 minutes for the core demo, 10–15 with Q&A
**Goal:** Get them to lean forward at the import moment, then ask "when can we get this?"

---

## BEFORE YOU START

**Setup checklist (do 30 minutes before):**

- Log in as an account on a tenant that has NOT yet imported anything (so the demo has a fresh "first import" moment). Recommended: create a dedicated demo tenant called something like "DemoBrokerage" for a clean slate.
- Make sure your user role is Sales Manager or Admin (so the import button is visible).
- Pull up two tabs in the browser — one on PropPulse, one on Inventory — to switch fast if the demo needs it.
- Close your email, Slack, WhatsApp Web. Anything that can ping.
- Have a backup: keep a screen-recorded version of the demo on your phone. If Wi-Fi dies at the office, you can still show the flow.
- Know your numbers cold: "23 verified UAE projects live right now. 20 developers. New ones added by our AI every week."

**What to wear / how to walk in:**

Tablet or laptop, not a phone. Brokers take tablets seriously. Sit next to them, not across from them — you want them looking at the screen with you, not at you.

---

## THE OPENING (60 SECONDS) — BEFORE YOU EVEN OPEN THE APP

Don't launch into features. Open with a question.

> "Before I show you anything — quick question. When a new Emaar project gets announced, how do you currently find out about it? And how long does it take before your agents have the brochure, the payment plan, and the commission structure ready to send to clients?"

Let them answer. They'll say some mix of: WhatsApp groups, developer emails, agents forwarding PDFs, hours of chasing. **Write down a number they give you — "three days" or "a week" — and use it later.**

Then:

> "What if every new project in the UAE — every tower Emaar announces, every launch Sobha does, every unit DAMAC puts on the market — was already in your CRM the day it was announced? Pre-loaded. With the brochure. With the commission rate. Ready for your agents to pitch."
>
> "That's PropPulse. Let me show you."

Open the laptop.

---

## SCENE 1 — THE CATALOG (60–90 SECONDS)

Click the **⚡ PropPulse** tab in the top nav.

The PropPulse catalog loads. 23 projects, 20 developers, live counters in the header.

**What to say while they look:**

> "This is every verified UAE project in the system right now. 23 live projects, 20 developers — Emaar, Sobha, DAMAC, Azizi, Binghatti, and so on. The counter at the top updates as our AI adds new ones. Every week there are more."
>
> "I can filter by emirate — [click Emirate filter, select Dubai] — Dubai only. I can filter by project status — [click Status filter, select Under Construction] — only under-construction towers. By developer, by project type, whatever I need."

Hover over a project row to show the hover state.

> "Each row shows the project, the community, type, units, starting price, handover date. And these icons — [point at 📍 and 📄] — take me straight to the map and the brochure."

Click one of the Sobha or Emaar projects (pick one that has a good description and all fields populated). The detail modal opens.

---

## SCENE 2 — THE DETAIL MODAL (45 SECONDS)

The project detail modal is open.

**What to say:**

> "Here's everything about the project. Starting price, total units, handover, community, announcement date, service charge. The brochure is one click — [click 📄 Brochure if it has one, show that it opens, then close the new tab] — and the map is one click — [point at 📍 View on Maps]."
>
> "But here's the part that changes the game."

Point at the **📥 Import to My Inventory** button (it sits at the right of the button row, dark navy, stands out).

> "I'm a sales manager at this brokerage. Watch what happens when I click this."

**PAUSE. Make them look. This is the moment.**

---

## SCENE 3 — THE IMPORT MOMENT (30 SECONDS — THE KEY MOMENT)

Click **📥 Import to My Inventory**.

The button changes to "⏳ Importing…". Within about a second, a green toast appears at the top: _"✅ Imported [Project Name] + [N] units to your inventory"_.

**Don't say anything for two seconds.** Let the toast land. The broker should be looking at it.

Then:

> "That just happened. That project is now in my brokerage's inventory. With all 10 units, all the pricing, all the data. Let me prove it."

Close the modal (click the ×).

The project row in PropPulse now shows a blue "📥 In My Inventory" badge.

> "See that? PropPulse is telling me I've already imported this one. I can't accidentally import it twice. The system knows."

---

## SCENE 4 — THE PROOF (60 SECONDS)

Click the **🏠 Inventory** tab in the top nav.

The Main Inventory view loads. The imported project and its units are now in the list.

> "This is my brokerage's inventory. My agents open this tab every day. And there's the project I just imported — [scroll to it] — with every single unit already populated. Bedrooms, size, floor, view, pricing. Ready to pitch."

Click into one of the imported units. The unit detail view opens.

> "From here, the sales cycle you already have takes over. My agent can assign this unit to a lead, create an opportunity, send a proposal, request a discount approval from me, book a reservation, generate the sales contract, track payments — the whole cycle. Same as any other unit in their book."
>
> "But they didn't chase a brochure for three days to get it there. It took me one click."

Pause. Tie back to their opening answer.

> "You said it takes your team [their number — three days / a week / whatever they said] today. With PropPulse it's thirty seconds."

---

## SCENE 5 — THE MULTI-TENANT PROOF (30 SECONDS, ONLY IF ASKED)

Only do this if a broker asks "but does my data stay mine?" or "can my competitors see my inventory?" — this is a very common question, and the answer should be ready.

> "Your data is yours. Full stop. Every brokerage on PropCRM has their own private space. I'll show you."

Click back to **⚡ PropPulse**. Then back to **🏠 Inventory**.

> "PropPulse — the shared catalog. Every brokerage sees this. It's a service we provide."
>
> "Inventory — your brokerage's private space. Only your agents, your manager, and you see this. I could show the same project to you now and to the brokerage next door, and once we each import it, we each have our own copy. Your pricing, your notes, your reservations, your contracts — they stay with you."

Optional technical reassurance, if they're the technical type:

> "At the database level, every row is tagged with your company ID. The system won't let anyone outside your company see your inventory. Even our AI can't — it only reads the public catalog."

## SCENE 6 — THE CLOSE (30 SECONDS)

> "Here's what's in it for your brokerage. Three things."
>
> "One — your agents stop chasing brochures. That time goes back into selling."
>
> "Two — you don't miss launches. Every new project in the market shows up in your CRM as soon as our AI catches it. Your agents pitch it the same day, not three weeks later when someone forwards the email."
>
> "Three — one tool. Leads, inventory, pipeline, commissions, reservations, contracts. And now market intelligence. Everything your team needs in one place, one login, one monthly fee."
>
> "I'm doing a small group of brokerages this month as our first cohort. Would you like to be one of them?"

**Then stop talking.** Let them respond.

## OBJECTIONS — HAVE THESE READY

**"What if two people at my brokerage import the same project?"**
> "The second person sees it's already in your inventory — the button is disabled. No duplicates. Clean."

**"Can I edit the project after importing?"**
> "Yes — once it's in your inventory it's yours. You can change pricing, add notes, update the status. The PropPulse catalog copy stays untouched; your copy is independent."

**"What if PropPulse updates the project? Does my copy update too?"**
> "Not automatically — and that's by design. Once you've imported, your version is yours. If the developer changes the starting price and PropPulse picks that up, you see it in PropPulse but your inventory copy stays exactly as you imported it. You decide whether to re-import or edit. This protects your deals in progress from surprise changes."
> *(Technical note for you: this is true today. A "sync updates" feature is a post-demo enhancement.)*

**"How does the AI find new projects?"**
> "It monitors developer websites, RERA Dubai, DLD listings, and announcements. It deduplicates, scores confidence, and only shows verified entries in PropPulse. Unverified ones go through a review queue before they ever appear."
> *(Technical note for you: is_pp_verified=true is the filter. Only verified rows show.)*

**"What happens to the inventory I already have? Do I have to rebuild?"**
> "Nothing changes. Your existing projects and units stay exactly as they are. PropPulse is additive — it gives you new projects you didn't have before. You can keep adding your own inventory manually too, exactly like you do today."

**"Can my agents import, or only I can?"**
> "You decide. Today the import button shows up for Sales Manager, Leasing Manager, and Admin roles. Individual agents can see PropPulse and browse the catalog, but import is a management decision. If you want to open it up to agents too, that's a setting we can change for you."

**"What does this cost?"**
> Whatever you've decided. Hold the number until they ask, don't lead with it. If pressed earlier, deflect with "let me finish showing you what you get, then we'll talk pricing — it's reasonable."

**"How long to onboard my brokerage?"**
> "Two hours. We set up your tenant, migrate any existing inventory you have in a spreadsheet, train your team on a call, and you're live. PropPulse data is already there on day one."

**"What if I have 500 units already in Excel?"**
> "We import it for you. There's an Excel upload inside Inventory — I can show you — but for onboarding we handle it for you so you're live faster."

**"My agents work on WhatsApp. Does this integrate?"**
> "The unit cards and project summaries are WhatsApp-ready — your agent hits share and it's in the chat with the client. Full WhatsApp messaging integration is on our roadmap."
> *(Technical note: don't overcommit — say "on the roadmap" only if you mean it.)*

## PROVING "THE INVENTORIES ARE THE SAME"

This is the part you specifically asked about — how to convince brokers that PropPulse and Main Inventory are structurally identical, so imports feel seamless.

You don't need to lecture them on schema. You prove it by showing three things side by side.

**Side-by-side proof (takes 30 seconds):**

1. In PropPulse, click a project → the detail modal shows: Starting Price, Total Units, Handover, Community, Announcement Date, Service Charge, Status, Type, Emirate, Description, Brochure, Map link.

2. Close the modal. Go to Inventory. Click any existing project → the detail shows: **the same fields, the same layout, the same data types.**

3. Say: _"You've noticed already — they're the same project card, same fields, same everything. That's because they are. PropPulse is the discovery side; Inventory is the working side. The import isn't a transformation — it's a copy into your private space."_

**Why this works:** brokers don't care about databases. They care that when they import, nothing gets lost, nothing gets weird, nothing needs re-entering. The visual similarity between the two views is the proof.

---

## IF SOMETHING BREAKS LIVE

If the import fails, if a toast doesn't appear, if a page freezes — **don't panic and don't apologise repeatedly.**

Say one of these:

> "The Wi-Fi is a little slow in here — let me try that again." [retry once]

> "Let me pull up the backup I recorded earlier." [play the phone video]

> "Bear with me one moment — this is why we build for reliability, not for demos." [recover, move on]

What NOT to say: "Oh no, it's broken," "This never happens," "Let me check the code." Brokers will forgive a glitch but they won't forgive you looking panicked.
